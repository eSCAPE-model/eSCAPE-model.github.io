What is it?
eSCAPE is a scalable parallelised Python-based numerical model built to simulate landscapes and basins evolution at high resolution.
For what purpose?
eSCAPE can address problems related to Earth surface dynamics such as geomorphology, hydrology, and stratigraphy, it can also be used in related fields.

About…

Apr 12, 2019

eSCAPE is a parallel TIN-based landscape evolution model, built to simulate topography dynamic at various space and time scales. The model accounts for hillslope processes (soil creep using linear diffusion), fluvial incision (stream power law), spatially and temporally varying tectonics (horizontal and vertical displacements) and climatic forces (temporal and spatial precipitation changes and/or sea-level fluctuations). The specs The model is based on the following approaches: an adaptation of the implicit, parallelizable method for calculating drainage area for both single (D8) and multiple flow direction (Dinf) from Richardson et al.
